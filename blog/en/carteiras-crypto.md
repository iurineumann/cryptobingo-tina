## What is a Crypto Wallet?

A crypto wallet does not "store" your cryptocurrencies — it stores the private keys that prove you own them. Think of it as a keychain: the key (private) opens the door and allows you to move what is inside, but the safe itself is on the blockchain.

Every wallet generates a key pair:
- **Public key:** your address to receive (like an account number)
- **Private key:** your master password to send (never share it)

## Types of Wallets

**Custodial Wallets:** a third party holds your private keys. Example: WAX Cloud Wallet. You access it with email and password. The company holds the key.

**Self-Custody:** you control your private keys. Example: Anchor Wallet. You have full control — and full responsibility.

**Hot Wallet vs Cold Wallet:** hot wallets stay connected to the internet (practical for gaming). Cold wallets are offline (safer for large amounts).

## WAX Cloud Wallet

Recommended for beginners. Works like any online account: email + password + verification. No seed phrase to manage. Creating an account takes 30 seconds.

**Pros:** Simple, recoverable by email, integrated with WAX ecosystem
**Cons:** You do not control the private key

## Anchor Wallet

Full self-custody. You generate and control your 12-word seed phrase. Supports multiple Antelope blockchains (WAX, EOS, Telos).

**Pros:** Full control, multi-chain support, open source
**Cons:** You are responsible for the seed phrase — losing it means losing access

## Which One to Choose?

| Criteria | WAX Cloud Wallet | Anchor Wallet |
|----------|------------------|---------------|
| Ease of use | Excellent (30s) | Moderate (5 min) |
| Control | Custodial | Self-custody |
| Seed phrase | Not needed | 12 words |
| Recovery | Email + password | Seed phrase |
| Best for | Beginners, gaming | Advanced users |

**Our recommendation:** start with WAX Cloud Wallet. Play, get comfortable. When you have more tokens or want more control, migrate to Anchor.

## Security Tips

- **Seed phrase:** never digitize it. Write it on paper and store it in a safe place. A physical safe is even better.
- **2FA:** enable two-factor authentication whenever available.
- **Fake sites:** always verify the URL before connecting your wallet.
- **Never share private keys:** no legitimate service will ask for your seed phrase.
